Siteswap Juggling

The applet below demonstrates siteswap juggling patterns.

What are the numbers?

Each number corresponds with the time the ball spends in the air. For example, a 5 throw will be thrown again 5 beats later, a 4 throw will be thrown again 4 beats later. The numbers are called siteswaps.

To juggle three balls in the cascade pattern (the simplest 3 ball pattern) each ball is thrown every three beats (allowing for two throws in the mean time). Therefore the pattern is given by "3".

Even numbers land in the same hand, and odd numbers will land in the other hand.

The number of beats is the number of numbers which describe the pattern. For example, the pattern "441" has three beats, the pattern "3" has one beat.
